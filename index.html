1.Introduction

There are three distinct types of distribution of data:-

1) Population Distribution characterizes the distribution of elements of a population

2) Sample Distribution characterizes the distribution of elements of a sample drawns from a population

3) Sampling Distribution describes the expected behaviour of a large number of simple random samples drawn from the same population

2.What is Sampling Distribution?

Sampling Distribution of a statistic is a type of probability distribution created by drawing many random samples of a given size from the same population. These distributions help you understand how a sample statistic varies from sample to sample.

3.Types of Errors

Type-I
A type I error (false-positive) occurs if an investigator rejects a null hypothesis that is actually true in the population;

Type-II
type II error (false-negative) occurs if the investigator fails to reject a null hypothesis that is actually false in the population.

6.Concepts used in sampling

a. Universe or population

b. Stratum

c. Elements

d. Sample

Universe or population:
In sample language, a population or universe can be defined as any collection of persons or objects or event in which one is interested.
Stratum:Stratum can also be called as a sub population. A stratum may be defined by one or more specifications that divide a population into mutually exclusive segments.

Elements: By a population element we mean the units that make the population. Such units may be an individual, an object, or even a small group.

Sample: By sample we mean the aggregate of objects, persons or elements, selected from the universe. It is a portion or sub part of the total population..

8.Conclusion

Sampling errors are differences between a sample’s values and the actual population’s values.

It is possible to avoid them if analysts carefully choose representative subsets of data from which to draw conclusions about the entire population. Factors including sample size and design, diversity in the population, and sampling percentage all contribute to sampling errors.
